Death Square: A Puzzle Game Now on iOS

Download Death Squared (RORORORO) on the App Store

This game, perfect for one or two players, combines teamwork with robot explosions. Using two controllers, one player will control the red robot, and the other will control the blue robot. You must work together through 80 levels, trying not to interfere with each other's robots. The voice acting adds a fun touch, and at $5.99, Death Square is well worth checking out. Please note that the game doesn't fit the full-screen resolution of the new iPad Pro.

Targa Check This Out

At number eight is Targa. Released on February 20, 2010, this action-fantasy game known as "light collision" supports local multiplayer for up to four players. You'll shoot beams to damage opponents, but watch your mana levels! Collect crystals to replenish mana and be careful of the dangerous purple mist. Who will be the winner?

Bad Land Check This Out

Don't forget about Bad Land at number seven! This original game supports up to four players on the same device. You can join co-op or vs. mode from the main menu, and connecting is as simple as pressing a button on the controller. Play the single-player campaign in co-op or compete against each other in specially designed levels. Bad Land was one of the earliest games on the App Store, but note that it doesn't fit full-screen mode.

 

Community Soccer: A Casual Sports Game Check This Out

Featuring arcade-style play, Community Soccer offers friendly matches for two players. With connected controllers, both players can choose teams and go head-to-head in quick, action-packed matches. The game's movement may feel awkward at times, but it's highly addictive and great for a fun time with friends. Don't take it too seriously!

Decision Making: A Game for Any Skill Level Check This Out

Whether playing with a younger sibling or a seasoned gamer, this game provides good fun without stressing over the outcome. Available for $6.99 USD on the App Store, it's a light and enjoyable game for any occasion.

Octodad: A Hilarious Cooperative Experience Check This Out

Octodad is a quirky game that allows local co-op for up to four players. Assign different parts of the octopus's body to each controller and perform tasks to hide the octopus's identity. Be warned, this game might test your friendship, causing laughter, arguments, or great teamwork. It's a desperate catch of fun!

Cat Crest: Open-World Action RPG Check This Out

Cat Crest is an open-world action RPG set in a fantasy realm of cats. With two controllers connected, players can enjoy co-op gameplay and explore different weapons, spells, and abilities. Whether attacking or defending, working together in this cat-filled adventure is an engaging experience.

Hardcore: A Rhythm Game with Local Co-Op Check This Out

This rhythm-based game offers a unique challenge where players must move and attack to the beat. Join a friend in local cooperative play and explore various tasks, unlock weapons, and more. Please note that the game doesn't fit the full-screen resolution of the new iPad Pro screens.

Road to Canada: Action RPG Check This Out

At number one is Death Road to Canada, an action RPG focused on surviving the zombie apocalypse. With a diverse array of locations and supplies to find, including melee weapons and food, it offers a rich and replayable gaming experience. Connect controllers and choose player characters to start the game. The varied gameplay makes it a standout option on this list.
